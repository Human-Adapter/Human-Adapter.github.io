Human-Adapter: Personalized Multimodal 3D Human Generation with Gaussian Splatting

Zhibin Liu, Haoye Dong, Hefeng W, Hefeng Wu, Zhenyu Xie, Ruomei Wang, Xiaodan Liang, Liang Lin
Sun Yat-Sen University,Carnegie Mellon University
Paper Supplementary Code arXiv



Abstract

Remarkable advancements have been achieved in 3D human generation, with 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) emerging as a promising generation technique. However, existing 3D human generation methods apply 3DGS heavily based on multi-view images or videos, remaining challenges in preserving identity and controlling shape. To address these issues, we introduce a shape and appearance adaption framework, named \textbf{Human-Adapter}, which generates personalized 3D human without multi-view images, controlling 3D human shape by using user measurements. We design a specialized measurement-to-shape module that intricately controls body shape with measurements, which directly boosts the initiation of 3DGS. We establish a connection between the 3DGS-generated human and the user's identity image. This is achieved by progressively guiding 3DGS optimization with adapted 2D view projections with multimodal input, employing a personalized guidance diffusion model that links human appearance to the face image and text prompt. Furthermore, we employ the decoupled cross-attention mapping and a face refinement strategy for keeping the person's identity. Human-Adapter makes the first attempt to achieve not only controlling body shape but also keeping identity with a specified face image. Experiments demonstrate that we achieve promising 3D humans with shape control and identity preservation.






To better evaluate the performance of our framework, we demonstrate more visualized generation results compared with state-of-the-art (SOTA) works.




To verify the efficiency of the Measurement-to-Shape Module, we demonstrate six SMPL-X meshes generated from six distinguishing measurements.
